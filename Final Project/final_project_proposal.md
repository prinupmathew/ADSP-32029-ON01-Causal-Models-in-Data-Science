Final Project Proposal

Overview:
For your final project, you will conduct a comprehensive causal analysis using one of the public datasets (CriteoLinks to an external site., etc.). The goal of this proposal is to outline your analytical plan and demonstrate a clear understanding of the causal inference techniques you intend to apply.

Objective:

In the proposal, you should identify a specific causal question of interest and describe how you plan to use multiple causal inference methods to estimate treatment effects and validate your findings. This proposal will help ensure your project is well-structured, methodologically sound, and feasible within the course timeline.

Requirements:

Your proposal (no more than four pages) should include the following sections:

1. Causal Question and Motivation

Clearly define your causal question (e.g., “What is the effect of an intervention on an outcome?”).

Explain why this question is meaningful in the context of the chosen dataset.

2. Dataset Selection and Description

State which dataset you plan to use (IHDP, Twins, Lalonde, Criteo, etc.).

Briefly describe its key variables and relevance to your causal question.

3. Proposed Methods

Specify at least two causal inference techniques you plan to apply.

Explain why each method is appropriate for your question and how you will compare or validate results across methods.

4. Evaluation Plan

Describe how you will assess the robustness and credibility of your causal estimates.

5. Expected Challenges and Next Steps

Anticipate potential data or methodological issues and how you plan to address them.

Outline your next steps toward completing the full analysis.
